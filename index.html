07 March 2023/Science Blogs Quantum Entanglement

For us living in a classical world, particles and waves form different pictures in our minds. Particles have mass and a well-defined position,whereas waves are carriers of energy and are delocalized. When two waves collide, they do not bounce off each other like particles, but instead interfere with each other. However, these distinctions seem to break down as we peek into the world on a quantum scale. It has been observed that light and matter have properties of both particles and waves, but both of these properties cannot be observed simultaneously.

07 March 2023/Science Blogs Black Holes and Degeneracy Pressure

The question of why all stars don't turn into black holes at the end of their lives is a fascinating one that leads us to delve into the principles of quantum mechanics and the forces at play within stars. Throughout a star's life, it undergoes a constant battle between two fundamental forces: gravity, which pulls matter inward, and the pressure generated by nuclear reactions in its core, which pushes matter outward. This balance allows a star to maintain its stability and prevent collapse.
